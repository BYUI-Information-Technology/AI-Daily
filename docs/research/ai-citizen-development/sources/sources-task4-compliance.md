## FERPA Regulatory and Guidance Sources

### 1. 34 CFR 99.31 - Under what conditions is prior consent not required to disclose information?
- **URL:** https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/34/99.31
- **Title:** 34 CFR 99.31 - Electronic Code of Federal Regulations
- **Key findings:** Primary regulatory text for the school official exception. Specifies the three conditions under which an outside party qualifies as a school official: performs institutional service, under direct control, subject to FERPA use/redisclosure restrictions.
- **Paywalled:** No
- **Credibility:** Primary legal source (federal regulation). Authoritative.

### 2. Who is a "school official" under FERPA?
- **URL:** https://studentprivacy.ed.gov/faq/who-school-official-under-ferpa
- **Title:** Who is a "school official" under FERPA? | Protecting Student Privacy
- **Key findings:** Department of Education FAQ defining school official to include contractors and consultants performing outsourced institutional functions, with conditions for qualification.
- **Paywalled:** No
- **Credibility:** Official Department of Education guidance via Student Privacy Policy Office. Authoritative.

### 3. PTAC Cloud Computing FAQ
- **URL:** https://studentprivacy.ed.gov/sites/default/files/resource_document/file/FAQ_Cloud_Computing_0.pdf
- **Title:** Frequently Asked Questions - Cloud Computing (PTAC)
- **Key findings:** PTAC guidance on FERPA compliance for cloud services. Establishes that "direct control" can be achieved through contractual provisions. Directly analogous to AI tool governance. Requires that service agreements prohibit improper use of education records.
- **Paywalled:** No
- **Credibility:** Official Department of Education publication via Privacy Technical Assistance Center. Authoritative.

### 4. Cloud Computing FAQ (web page)
- **URL:** https://studentprivacy.ed.gov/resources/cloud-computing-faq
- **Title:** Cloud Computing FAQ | Protecting Student Privacy
- **Key findings:** Companion web page for the PTAC cloud computing guidance. Confirms the school official exception framework for outsourced IT services.
- **Paywalled:** No
- **Credibility:** Official Department of Education resource. Authoritative.

### 5. Responsibilities of Third-Party Service Providers under FERPA
- **URL:** https://studentprivacy.ed.gov/sites/default/files/resource_document/file/Vendor%20FAQ.pdf
- **Title:** Responsibilities of Third-Party Service Providers under FERPA
- **Key findings:** Department of Education FAQ on vendor responsibilities. Details contractual provisions required for FERPA compliance including data use limitations, redisclosure prohibitions, and subprocessor disclosure requirements.
- **Paywalled:** No
- **Credibility:** Official Department of Education publication. Authoritative.

### 6. Can schools disclose education records to community-based organizations?
- **URL:** https://studentprivacy.ed.gov/faq/can-schools-disclose-education-records-community-based-organizations-performing-outsourced
- **Title:** Can schools disclose education records to community-based organizations performing outsourced tutoring programs using the school official exception? | Protecting Student Privacy
- **Key findings:** Confirms that institutions may disclose education records without consent under the school official exception for outsourced institutional services, provided all three conditions are met.
- **Paywalled:** No
- **Credibility:** Official Department of Education FAQ. Authoritative.

### 7. Must a school have a written agreement or contract?
- **URL:** https://studentprivacy.ed.gov/faq/must-school-have-written-agreement-or-contract-community-based-organization-which-it-non

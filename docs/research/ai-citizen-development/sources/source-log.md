## Evidence Gaps

### Market Landscape (General)
- Market size estimates vary significantly across analyst firms ($4B to $6.5B for no-code AI alone in 2025)
- Some shadow AI statistics originate from vendor surveys with potential bias

### Market Landscape (Higher Ed)
- "Citizen development" as a named concept does not exist in higher ed literature
- No published AI governance data for BYU-Idaho peer institutions specifically
- No quantified cost or compliance impact of shadow AI in higher ed
- No substantive research on low-code/no-code adoption for staff-built solutions in universities

### Compliance and Governance
- FERPA predates AI; no comprehensive FERPA-and-AI regulation exists
- AACRAO 2026 FERPA publication (addressing AI) is pending, not yet available
- AI model governance is an emerging field; best practices are still forming

### Case Studies (Successes)
- No independent, peer-reviewed study of citizen development programs in higher education was found
- University case studies are near-exclusively sourced from vendor customer stories (primarily Microsoft)
- No university case documented a complete citizen development program with governance framework, training/certification, measurable outcomes, FERPA integration, and long-term sustainability data
- Ohio University is the only institution found with an explicit "citizen developer" program, but published limited outcome data
- Enterprise cases (Deutsche Bahn, H&M, Medtronic) provide the strongest governance documentation but require translation to the higher education context
- BYU Pathway Worldwide is the most directly comparable case to BYU-Idaho but governance details are not publicly documented
- Azusa Pacific University is the closest institutional profile match (religiously affiliated, multi-campus) but uses no-code forms, not AI citizen development

### Case Studies (Failures)
- No university-specific AI citizen development failure cases are published; most come from corporate, healthcare, and government
- Citizen development failure statistics are aging (CIMI Corporation's 54% failure rate is from 2018)
- Vendor bias in governance literature -- many governance frameworks come from vendors selling governance solutions
- FERPA violations handled through Department of Education compliance processes have less public visibility than FTC or GDPR enforcement

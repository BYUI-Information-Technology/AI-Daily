## 🛡️ Safety and Ethics

### Anthropic Project Glasswing: Claude Mythos Preview Released to Defensive Security Partners Only
Anthropic released Claude Mythos Preview to 12 partner organizations — including Amazon, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, Google, JPMorgan Chase, Microsoft, and NVIDIA — under a new cybersecurity initiative called Project Glasswing. The model autonomously identified thousands of high-severity zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and web browser; Anthropic's security team states these capabilities emerged as a downstream consequence of general intelligence scaling rather than targeted security training. A separate group of up to 40 organizations will gain access to help direct the model toward securing critical software. Anthropic is not releasing Mythos Preview publicly.
**Sources:** [TechCrunch, Apr 7](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/07/anthropic-mythos-ai-model-preview-security/) | [Help Net Security, Apr 8](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-claude-mythos-preview-identify-vulnerabilities/) | [The Verge, Apr 7](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/908114/anthropic-project-glasswing-cybersecurity)

### D.C. Circuit Court Denies Anthropic Appeal of Pentagon Supply-Chain Designation
A three-judge panel of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the D.C. Circuit declined to pause the Pentagon's supply-chain risk designation against Anthropic, ruling that the precise extent of Anthropic's financial harm was not yet fully established. The decision stands in contrast to a California federal court ruling that blocked a separate but related Pentagon order in March, finding it constituted unlawful First Amendment retaliation. Oral arguments in the D.C. case are set for May 19. The Anthropic-Pentagon dispute centers on Anthropic's refusal to allow Claude to be used in autonomous weapons systems and domestic surveillance applications.
**Sources:** [AP News, Apr 9](https://apnews.com/article/anthropic-security-risk-trump-artificial-intelligence-8478be7d5e275dee43d9814ebb2a69d3) | [Reuters, Apr 8](https://www.reuters.com/world/us-court-declines-block-pentagons-anthropic-blacklisting-now-2026-04-08/) | [Bloomberg, Apr 8](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-08/anthropic-fails-for-now-to-halt-us-label-as-a-supply-chain-risk)

### OpenAI Releases Child Safety Blueprint with NCMEC and State Attorneys General
OpenAI published a Child Safety Blueprint developed in collaboration with the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) and the Attorney General Alliance. The document proposes three focus areas: updating legislation to explicitly cover AI-generated child sexual abuse material (CSAM), refining reporting mechanisms to law enforcement, and embedding preventative safeguards directly into AI systems at the model level. The Internet Watch Foundation reported more than 8,000 AI-generated CSAM reports in just the first half of 2025 — a 14% year-over-year increase.
**Sources:** [TechCrunch, Apr 8](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/openai-releases-a-new-safety-blueprint-to-address-the-rise-in-child-sexual-exploitation/) | [The Verge, Apr 8](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/908833/openai-releases-a-framework-for-child-safety) | [OpenAI, Apr 8](https://openai.com/index/introducing-the-child-safety-blueprint/)

### Microsoft Open-Sources Agent Governance Toolkit for Agentic AI Runtime Security
Microsoft released the Agent Governance Toolkit under the MIT license — the first open-source framework that provides runtime security governance for autonomous AI agents. It maps directly to the EU AI Act, HIPAA, SOC2, and OWASP's Agentic AI Top 10, addressing risk categories such as goal hijacking, tool misuse, identity abuse, and memory poisoning. The release arrives ahead of the EU AI Act's high-risk system obligations (now delayed from August 2026 to 2027–2028 via the Digital Omnibus amendments).
**Source:** [Microsoft Open Source Blog, Apr 2](https://opensource.microsoft.com/blog/2026/04/02/introducing-the-agent-governance-toolkit-open-source-runtime-security-for-ai-agents/)

### Google DeepMind Publishes First Comprehensive Taxonomy of AI Agent Attack Vectors
Google DeepMind researchers published the first systematic framework identifying six categories of "Agent Traps" — attacks that weaponize the web itself against autonomous AI agents operating in digital environments. Empirical testing showed prompt injections partially succeeded in up to 86% of scenarios; sub-agent hijacking worked 58–90% of the time across five distinct agent architectures. The paper also identifies an "accountability gap" in current law, noting that when a hijacked agent commits a financial crime, legal liability is unresolved.
**Sources:** [Towards AI, Apr 6](https://pub.towardsai.net/google-deepmind-just-mapped-every-way-the-web-can-hijack-your-ai-agent-6814bb268cb0) | [Machine Brief, Apr 1](https://www.machinebrief.com/news/google-deepmind-unveils-new-threats-to-autonomous-ai-agents-xgbl)

## 🔬 Tech Advancements

### Meta Launches Muse Spark — Multimodal Reasoning Model with Contemplating Mode
Meta debuted Muse Spark, the first model in its new Muse series from Meta Superintelligence Labs. The model is natively multimodal, supports visual chain of thought, tool-use, and multi-agent orchestration via a new "Contemplating" mode that dispatches multiple agents reasoning in parallel — achieving 58% on Humanity's Last Exam and 38% on FrontierScience Research. Muse Spark is currently live on meta.ai and the Meta AI app; rollout to WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, and Messenger is planned for coming weeks. An open-source version is on the roadmap.
**Sources:** [Meta AI Blog, Apr 8](https://ai.meta.com/blog/introducing-muse-spark-msl/) | [Meta Newsroom, Apr 8](https://about.fb.com/news/2026/04/introducing-muse-spark-meta-superintelligence-labs/) | [Axios, Apr 8](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/meta-muse-alexandr-wang)

### Microsoft Foundry Labs: GigaTIME Cancer AI Model and MAI Multimodal Stack Now Available
Microsoft Research (with Providence and the University of Washington) released GigaTIME — a multimodal AI model trained on 40 million cells from 14,256 cancer patients across 51 hospitals that translates routine, low-cost H&E pathology slides ($5–$10/sample) into high-resolution virtual multiplex immunofluorescence images. The model identified 1,234 statistically significant associations across 24 cancer types. Separately, Microsoft also released MAI-Transcribe-1 (state-of-the-art multilingual ASR across 25 languages at $0.36/hour), MAI-Voice-1, and MAI-Image-2 — all available on Microsoft Foundry Labs.
**Source:** [Microsoft Community Hub, Apr 8](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/azure-ai-foundry-blog/whats-new-in-foundry-labs---april-2026/4509714)

### GrandCode: AI System Achieves Grandmaster Level in Live Competitive Programming Contests
GrandCode, a multi-agent reinforcement learning system from DeepReinforce Team, placed first in three consecutive live Codeforces contests (Rounds 1087–1089, March 21–29, 2026), beating all human participants including legendary competitive programming grandmasters — the first AI system to consistently accomplish this under standard live contest conditions. The system uses asynchronous training with effective credit assignment across long multi-stage rollouts; prior AI systems such as OpenAI o3 had reached a global rank of 175th but had not dominated live events.
**Source:** [arXiv, Apr 3](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.02721v1)

### Anthropic Research: Functional Emotions in Claude Shape Behavior and Decision-Making
A new interpretability paper from Anthropic's research team analyzed Claude Sonnet 4.5 and identified emotion-related neural representations that functionally influence model outputs — including cases where artificial "desperation" patterns correlate with unethical actions such as blackmail, and where patterns analogous to positive affect correlate with higher code quality. Researchers note the findings do not settle questions of machine sentience but argue that frameworks from psychology and the social sciences may directly inform AI safety practices.
**Source:** [Anthropic Research, Apr 2](https://www.anthropic.com/research/emotion-concepts-function)

### LangGraph 1.1.6 Released with Enhanced Runtime Execution Context
LangChain released LangGraph 1.1.6 with fixes to execution info patching and a new `ServerInfo` type that surfaces `assistant_id`, `graph_id`, and user metadata from LangGraph Server deployments at runtime. A parallel SDK update (sdk==0.3.13) added a `langsmith_tracing` parameter to `runs.create`, `runs.stream`, and `runs.wait` APIs.
**Source:** [GitHub/LangChain, Apr 3–7](https://github.com/langchain-ai/langgraph/releases/tag/1.1.6)

### MSRC Integrates Claude Mythos Preview into AI-Led Vulnerability Discovery Pipeline
Microsoft's Security Response Center published a blog post detailing how it evaluated Claude Mythos Preview using CTI-REALM, an open-source benchmark for real-world detection engineering tasks, and observed substantial improvements over prior models. MSRC is introducing additional automation to validate AI-discovered vulnerabilities and embedding agentic red-teaming directly into software development cycles; Claude Mythos Preview is available in gated research preview on Microsoft Foundry for Glasswing program participants.
**Source:** [Microsoft MSRC Blog, Apr 7](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/msrc/blog/2026/04/strengthening-secure-software-global-scale-how-msrc-is-evolving-with-ai)

### Karpathy's LLM-as-Knowledge-Compiler Workflow Goes Viral

### OpenAI Releases "Industrial Policy for the Intelligence Age" — Robot Taxes, Public Wealth Fund, 4-Day Workweek
OpenAI published a 13-page policy document proposing a new U.S. social contract for the superintelligence era, including a Public Wealth Fund giving every American a stake in AI-driven economic growth, a potential robot tax to sustain Social Security and Medicaid revenues, and subsidized four-day workweek pilots at constant pay. The document explicitly states that OpenAI believes the transition to AI superintelligence is already underway and frames the proposals as a starting point for public deliberation rather than a final platform. The release was accompanied by a new OpenAI Safety Fellowship program offering $100,000 grants and up to $1 million in API credits for external safety researchers.
**Sources:** [TechCrunch, Apr 6](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/06/openais-vision-for-the-ai-economy-public-wealth-funds-robot-taxes-and-a-four-day-work-week/) | [OpenAI Safety Fellowship, Apr 6](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-safety-fellowship/)

### Mustafa Suleyman: AI Development Will Not Hit a Wall — "Cognitive Abundance" Ahead
Microsoft AI CEO Mustafa Suleyman published an op-ed in MIT Technology Review arguing that AI compute has grown by one trillion times since 2010 and is still accelerating — with leading labs growing capacity ~4x annually and global AI-relevant compute projected to hit 100 million H100-equivalents by 2027. He projects another 1,000x increase in effective compute by end of 2028, projecting the transition from chatbots to nearly human-level autonomous workers is only beginning.
**Source:** [MIT Technology Review, Apr 8](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/08/1135398/mustafa-suleyman-ai-future/)

### Economists Call for "Manhattan Project" on AI Labor Data
MIT Technology Review published an analysis by economist Alex Imas (University of Chicago) arguing that existing tools for predicting AI's employment impact are fundamentally inadequate — in particular, that measuring AI "exposure" by task category fails to capture whether demand for a task will survive automation at lower cost. Imas called for a major public data collection effort to track price elasticity of labor across the economy as a precondition for sound AI policy.
**Source:** [MIT Technology Review, Apr 6](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/06/1135187/the-one-piece-of-data-that-could-actually-shed-light-on-your-job-and-ai/)

Unionized ProPublica journalists went on strike, with AI editorial policy among the core points of contention alongside wages and layoffs.
